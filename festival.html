Chuseok Festival

Chuseok (추석), also known as Korean Thanksgiving Day, is one of the most important traditional holidays in Korea. It is celebrated on the 15th day of the 8th month of the lunar calendar, usually falling in September or early October. During Chuseok, families come together to honor their ancestors, share food, and celebrate the harvest season.

Shown belor are the traditional foods that are prepared for the ancestral rites table, where family will gather together day before the chuseok festival to prepare the food offerings and set up the table for the ancestral rites ceremony.

Seollal Festival

Seollal (설날), the Korean Lunar New Year, is one of the most significant traditional holidays in Korea. It marks the beginning of the lunar calendar year and is usually celebrated in late January or early February. During Seollal, families gather to perform ancestral rites, wear traditional hanbok clothing, play folk games, and enjoy special foods like tteokguk (rice cake soup), which for their beliefs, eating tteokguk on the new year actually adds another year to their age, which shows why the korean age system are different than other countries. Example, people who are born in 2003 should've be 22 in 2025, but in Korea, their age will be added by one so they will be 23 in Korea.
