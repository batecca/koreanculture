Intro to Hangeul

Korean alphabet, Hangeul (한글) which consists of 24 letters that combines 14 consonants and 10 vowels that are arranged into a syllable blocks. It was created in the 15th century, year 1443, by King Sejong the Great to promote literacy among common people.

Common Phrases

  • Hello: 안녕하세요 (Annyeonghaseyo)
  • Thank you: 감사합니다 (Gamsahamnida)
  • Yes: 네 (Ne)
  • No: 아니요 (Aniyo)
  • Please: 제발 (Jebal)
  • Excuse me/Sorry: 죄송합니다 (Joesonghamnida)
  • Goodbye: 안녕히 가세요 (Annyeonghi gaseyo)

Writing System

Hangeul is known for its scientific design and ease of learning. Each letter is designed to represent the shape of the mouth and tongue when pronouncing the sound. For example, the consonant "ㄱ" (g/k) represents the shape of the tongue touching the back of the upper palate. You can also rarely hears the letter "R" in Korean phrases as it is hard for Korean to pronounce, instead of "R", you will probably hear them say it as the letter "L" instead which in hangeul "R" and "L" are actually one letter (ㄹ).
